# Catalyst Rubric

## Event Ledger

For each material item, capture:

- `event_time`: when the event happened or was published.
- `source`: official filing, regulator, exchange, company IR, reputable media, data provider, or social/rumor.
- `claim`: the factual claim in one sentence.
- `entity`: stock, company, sector, supply chain, or index affected.
- `impact_channel`: revenue, margin, order backlog, valuation, liquidity, policy support, risk appetite, supply-demand, or sentiment.
- `market_reaction`: price change, volume, limit-up/down status, turnover, relative sector/index move.
- `counterevidence`: dilution, already-priced move, weak confirmation, policy ambiguity, execution risk, valuation pressure, or broad-market drag.
- `missing_checks`: data that should be verified before increasing confidence.

## Catalyst Types

- Policy or regulation: national policy, ministry guidance, exchange rules, local subsidies, industry standards.
- Earnings and guidance: results, pre-announcement, forecast revision, margin change, cash flow, debt pressure.
- Orders and business progress: large contract, capacity expansion, product approval, license, partnership.
- Capital actions: buyback, dividend, insider increase/decrease, refinancing, M&A, restructuring.
- Capital flow and market structure: institutional flow, ETF or sector rotation, margin finance, northbound when available, block trades, limit-up board behavior.
- Sector theme: technology cycle, commodity price, supply squeeze, demand recovery, geopolitical or macro driver.
- Rumor or social sentiment: only useful after explicit verification; never treat as a primary fact.

## Scoring

Use a 0-100 score. The score is a decision aid, not a price forecast.

- Source reliability, 0-20: official or multi-source evidence scores high; anonymous or social-only evidence scores low.
- Materiality, 0-20: direct financial or regulatory impact scores high; vague theme association scores low.
- Immediacy, 0-10: near-term operational or trading impact scores high; long-cycle optionality scores lower.
- Novelty, 0-10: new information scores high; repeated or well-known information scores lower.
- Confirmation, 0-10: independent confirmation, filings, numbers, or data consistency scores high.
- Market alignment, 0-10: sector, index, volume, breadth, and peer reaction support the thesis.
- Price absorption, 0 to minus 10: subtract when the move appears crowded, already priced, or valuation-stretched.
- Counterevidence, 0 to minus 10: subtract for execution risk, policy uncertainty, financial weakness, or contradictory data.

Grades:

- 80-100: strong positive catalyst, still requires monitoring.
- 65-79: constructive catalyst, evidence is decent but not decisive.
- 50-64: mixed or weak positive catalyst.
- 35-49: low-confidence, mostly sentiment or already priced.
- 0-34: not bullish or likely negative.

## Report Template

Use Chinese unless the user asks otherwise:

1. Bottom line: bullish, bearish, mixed, or insufficient evidence.
2. Catalyst score and confidence: include `High`, `Medium`, or `Low`.
3. Evidence table: time, source, fact, impact channel, citation.
4. Why it may be bullish: 3-5 concise points.
5. Counterevidence and priced-in risk: 3-5 concise points.
6. Market validation: price-volume, sector/index, peers, fundamentals, and base-rate checks.
7. Watchlist: concrete future signals that would confirm or invalidate the thesis.
8. Caveat: not financial advice, no guaranteed return.
